Consolidated AI Synthesis

The authors present a compelling case for the role of interfacial water molecules in stabilizing the hydrophobic core of amyloid fibrils. Unlike previous models, this analysis suggests that hydration shells are not purely disruptive but provide a structural scaffold during initial nucleation phases (Rostova et al., 2024).

Key findings emphasize the rate-limiting transition between amorphous oligomers and highly ordered beta-sheet structures. This transition was monitored across a pH gradient from 5.5 to 7.4, revealing a critical instability window in lysosomal-like environments.
